A year ago I happily paid $20/month for my AI coding tools. Now I pay 5x that - and the rate card is still climbing.

Everyone's arguing about GitHub Copilot moving to usage-based billing. But that's the symptom. The real move is upstream: the per-token API price itself is going up.

I run an AI Werewolf side project that calls the APIs of every major provider, so I track their pricing release over release. Almost every model that shipped in April-May 2026 is more expensive than the one it replaced:

- OpenAI GPT-5.4 -> 5.5: +100% (doubled to $5/$30)
- Mistral Medium 3.1 -> 3.5: +275%
- Google Gemini Flash 3.1 -> 3.5: +200%
- Moonshot Kimi K2.5 -> K2.6: +46%
- Z.AI GLM-5 -> 5.1: +39% (roughly doubled over the past year)
- DeepSeek V3.2 -> V4 Pro: ~flat (spiked, then walked it back with a permanent 75% cut)

Mean increase: +74%. Median: +42%.

Two went the other way:
- xAI's Grok cut hard (-71%) - but quietly killed its cheapest coding-specific models in the process.
- Anthropic held Opus flat across three version bumps ($5/$25), even as its score on the DeepSWE coding benchmark roughly doubled. The one spot where the buyer got more for the same money.

And it's not just per-token. It's getting more expensive on three fronts at once:
- Tokens: the rate card itself, up despite better chips, bigger datacenters, and more efficient inference.
- Reasoning: nearly every model reasons by default now, burning more thinking tokens per task - tokens you often can't even audit.
- Harness: Claude Code became unusable on the $20 plan months ago. Comfortable coding now means $100/month. Codex went the same way.

The "Moore's Law for tokens" story - every release cheaper than the last - was a 2023-2024 thing. It's over.

So what do you do? I'm not giving up Claude Code. But I'm finally dusting off a 16GB AMD GPU to see how far local Qwen/Gemma/Mistral have come, just to keep a plan B alive. And here's the thing: companies are already complaining about their AI bills. The squeeze works right up until the people being squeezed start doing the math.
